Project Overview

Team: Group 8

Team Members: Giuseppe Scoppino, Charlotte Nadaud, Justin Mclendon, Lision Ajroni, Cody Engwer

Project: Language Learning App

Tagline: A language-learning app made specifically for teachers to teach young children a new language through interactive games.

Description:

We will be designing a language learning application for children in early education, with two focus points: vocabulary and phonology. For both focus points, there will be games for the children to play. The content used in the games can be chosen by the teacher, by using a content creator interface to input words and sentences that they wish to teach to their students, along with associated images and audio. The nature of the creator will allow for the app to be used to teach any language.

The challenge of our project will be to design an application that considers the needs of both the teacher and the student. The teacher will need to have a lot of control over which words and sounds to work with. Because the target audience of students is young (3-7 years old), the application will need to be designed such that they instinctively know how to use it without being able to read at a high level.

Target Users: Early Education Children, Early Education Instructors

Design Question: How can we provide a strong framework to teachers for teaching a new language to students, while also ensuring that the application is intuitive to students as well?

User Research Questions:

  1. How can the experience of forming words and sentences in a language learning app be improved?

  2. How can the experience of adding and managing content in a language learning app be improved?

  3. How can we design the Language Learning App to make it easy to use for young kids?

  4. How can we design the app to make it useful and compatible for classroom settings?

  5. How do we create an app similar to those such as Rosetta Stone, Duolingo, or Memrise, but centered around a significantly younger audience?

  • Created for User Centered Design in the Fall 2016.
